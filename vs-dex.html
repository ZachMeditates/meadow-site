What makes Juniper different

Built for friends, not deals

Dex is designed for professional networking: tracking contacts from conferences, following up after meetings, managing your LinkedIn connections. Juniper is designed for the people you actually care about. Your college roommate, your sister, the neighbor who watches your dog.

No data syncing needed

Dex auto-populates contacts from LinkedIn, Gmail, and your calendar. That's powerful for networking. Juniper takes a simpler approach: you tell it about your friends in plain language. No permissions to grant, no data pipelines to manage, no surprise when a work contact shows up in your personal CRM.

Juniper does the thinking

Dex stores what it finds and lets you set reminders. Juniper notices when you're losing touch, sends weekly briefs about who needs attention, and drafts personalized messages you can send with one tap. It tells you the when, the why, and the what to say.

Self-host for free

Dex is cloud-only with no self-hosted option. Juniper offers a free Docker image with SQLite. Run it on your own server, keep your data entirely under your control, and pay nothing. The hosted plan is there if you want convenience, not because you need it.

Where Dex still wins

We believe in honest comparisons. Here's where Dex has the edge.

LinkedIn integration

Dex automatically enriches contacts with data from LinkedIn. If you live on LinkedIn, that saves a lot of manual work that Juniper can't replicate.

Chrome extension

Dex lets you add contacts and take notes while browsing the web. Juniper lives in Telegram and doesn't have a browser extension.

Business networking

Dex is purpose-built for professional relationship management. If your goal is to maintain a large network of business contacts, Dex was designed for exactly that.

Calendar sync

Dex automatically logs meetings from your calendar. Juniper uses conversational input instead, which is more personal but requires you to tell it what happened.
