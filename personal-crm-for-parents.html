Personal CRM for parents

You're tracking 200+ relationships across school, sports, family, and the friendships you had before kids. You shouldn't need a spreadsheet to remember who's who.

The parent contact problem

Before kids, your social circle was manageable. You had your friends, your family, maybe some coworkers. You could keep most of it in your head.

Then your kid started school. Suddenly you're meeting 25 new families a year. Each one comes with names you need to remember, kids' names you definitely need to remember, birthdays, allergies, who drives which carpool, who volunteered for field day. Add sports and activities and the number doubles. Add a second kid and it doubles again.

Now layer your actual relationships on top of that. Your parents. Your siblings. Your college friends. The couple you used to have dinner with before everything got busy. The neighbor who helped you move in three years ago.

That's 200+ people. Nobody can hold all of that in their head. Something always falls through the cracks. You forget a birthday. You blank on a kid's name at pickup. You realize you haven't talked to your best friend from college in four months.

Why spreadsheets and apps don't work for parents

You've probably tried something. A spreadsheet. A contacts app. A notes file on your phone. Maybe you lasted a week.

The problem isn't the tool. The problem is that every one of them requires you to stop what you're doing, open an app, find the right contact, and fill in the details. That's fine if you're at a desk. But you're not at a desk. You're in the carpool line. You're at soccer practice. You're making dinner while your kid tells you about a birthday party next Saturday.

The tool has to meet you where you already are. If it requires a new habit, a new app, or more than 10 seconds of effort, it won't survive the first week of a busy school schedule.

How Juniper works for parents

Juniper lives in Telegram. You text it the way you'd text a friend, and it handles the rest.

Standing at the playground and a new parent introduces herself? Text Juniper: "met Jake's mom at soccer practice, her name is Lisa, she works at the hospital." Juniper creates the contact, links her to Jake, and notes where she works. Done. Five seconds, while your kid is still on the monkey bars.

A week later Lisa mentions something at pickup. Text Juniper: "Lisa said their anniversary trip to Italy is next month." It's saved. You didn't open an app. You didn't fill out a form. You just texted.

Every Monday, Juniper sends you a brief. It tells you who you haven't talked to in a while, reminds you about upcoming birthdays, and even drafts a message you can send. Not a generic template. A real opener based on what you actually know about that person.

What parents use Juniper for

  • Tracking school parent contacts and which kid goes with which family
  • Remembering kids' names, birthdays, and party details
  • Keeping up with coaches, teachers, and activity leaders
  • Staying in touch with friends from before kids
  • Managing family relationships across time zones
  • Remembering who volunteered for what and when

Juniper is a personal CRM that lives in your messaging app. No forms, no data entry, no new habit to build. Just text when you think of something.
